Civilian Casualties

Incident Code

ISPT0727

Incident date

October 29, 2023

Location

منزل عائلة الكرد في حي الجنينه برفح, al-Kurd Family Home, al-Jeneina Neighbourhood, Rafah, Gaza Strip, Rafah, the Gaza Strip
Web link

Airwars assessment

Shortly before 20:04 on Sunday, 29 October 2023, at least eight identified members of the al-Kurd family, including four women and two children, were killed and at least five others, including a young girl, were injured when their home near to the Awad Tower in the al-Jeneina neighbourhood of Rafah was reportedly hit by an Israeli airstrike.

Safa news agency was the first to report the incident at 20:04, writing about six “martyrs” from the attack on the al-Kurd family home. A Facebook account called Official journalist Adel Zourub shared photographs from the attack showing a totally destroyed building with many people attempting to move the rubble in the full darkness of the night to try and find any survivors. The photos also included one of a young girl on an operating table with her leg bandaged and covered in dust. However, her identity is not known at the time of writing.

Fatma Ḿ Ṧw Facebook post listed the names of the eight family members who had been killed in the attack. A post on Facebook from Abu Ismail Abdullah Ismail Al-Kurd also gave the names of eight people killed in the attack, as well as listing three injuries.

Zaki Shukri al-Kurd was injured in the strike, sustaining a fractured elbow. However, his 47-year-old wife Kifah Ismail Shukri al-Kurd was killed alongside her 44-year-old sister Abeer Ismail Saeed al-Kurd and their five children with Zaki – three daughters: 18-year-old Sondos Zaki Shukri al-Kurd, 21-year-old Nour Zaki Shukri al-Kurd and 16-year-old Shahed Zaki Shukri al-Kurd, and two sons: 10-year-old Muhammad Zaki Shukri al-Kurd and 14-year-old Abdullah Zaki Shukri al-Kurd.

Zaki’s brother, Saeed Shukri al-Kurd was injured, having a fractured femur, alongside his two sons, Ahmed Saeed Shukri al-Kurd and Mahmoud Saeed Shukri al-Kurd who were reportedly taken to intensive care. His third son, 21-year-old Shukri Saeed Shukri al-Kurd, lost his life in the attack.

Facebook user Sweet Palestine mourned the loss of his two aunts, Kifah and Abeer, and their children, and shared the collage of their photos.

All names have been matched with the Palestinian Ministry of Health list of fatalities in Gaza released on July 24th, 2024.

Where sources identified the belligerent, all sources attributed the strike to the Israeli military.

The incident occured at approximately 8:04 pm local time.
